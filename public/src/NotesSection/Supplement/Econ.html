4.9. Types of Consumption Goods

  • Durable Goods - It refers to those goods which can be used again and again over a considerable period of time
  • Semi Durable Goods - Goods which can be used for a limited period of time are termed as semi durable goods These goods have life span of around one year
  • Non Durable Goods - Goods which are used up in a single act of consumption are known as non durable goods These goods cannot be used more than once they loose their identity in single act of consumption
  • Service - It refers to non material goods which directly satisfy the human wants They are intangible activites they can neither been seen nor touched.

4.10. Capital goods

Capital goods are those final goods which help in production of other goods and services.

  • They are used in future for productive purposes and have expected life time of several years
  • They do not lose their identity in the production process. They do not get merged in the process of producction
  • They need repairs or replacement over time as they depreciate over a period of time
  • They have derived demand as their demand is derived from the demand for other goods, which they help to produce

4.18. Value Added Method

This method is used to measure national income in different phases of producction in the circular flow It shows the contribution of each producing unit in the production process

Concept of value added - refers to the addition of value to the raw material by a firm by virture of its productive activites. Value Added = Value Output - Intermediate consumption

Intermediate consumption refers to the expenditure incurred by a production unit on purchasing those goods and services for other production units, which are meant for resale or for using up completely during the same year

Final consumption refers to the expenditure on goods and services meant for final consumption and investment.

Value of output refers to the market value of all goods and services producced during a period of one year.

Value of Output = Sales + Change in Stock Change in stock = closing stock - Opening stock

Value of output = sales(quantity * price) + change in stock

Steps in Value added method

  1. identify and classify the production unit
  2. Estimate GDP at market price = sum of GVAmp
  3. Calculate domestic income NDPfc = GDPmp - Depreciation - Net indirect taxes

  4. Estimate NFIA to arrive at National INcome NNPfc = NDPfc + NFIA

    Sum of GVAmp of Primary, Secondary, Tertiary = GDPmp GDPmp - depreciation - net indirect taxes = NDPfc NDPfc + NFIA = NNPfc
